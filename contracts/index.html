Privacy

Your clients' data
never goes public.

Legal data is the most sensitive data there is. We built The Bull Contracts with a layered privacy architecture — so your contracts and your clients' information stay exactly where they belong.

Retrieval Isolation
We never send full contracts to the AI. Only the excerpts relevant to each query are retrieved — the rest of your document stays in our encrypted database, never exposed.
Firm-Scoped Workspaces
Every query is hard-filtered by your firm's ID at the database level. No data from another firm can ever appear in your workspace — by architecture, not policy.
Full Audit Trail
Every AI query, document upload, and automated action is logged with a timestamp and user identity. Full compliance evidence available on demand.
Zero Data Retention Coming soon
AI-provider level zero data retention — your prompts and contract content will never be logged or stored by any AI provider, disappearing the moment a response is returned.
Building toward SOC 2 Type II certification. All current controls are operational and enforceable by contract today. Enterprise clients receive a Data Processing Agreement (DPA) on request.
Web3 · Decentralized Party Network

Every party gets
their own window in.

Each contract gets a private peer network built on Web3 infrastructure. The lawyer spins it up in seconds, shares a link, and every party joins with their own cryptographic identity — seeing only what's theirs, communicating through a signed thread.

One Network Per Contract
The lawyer creates a dedicated peer network for each agreement. Contract data is published instantly — no server, no database, no intermediary. The network lives on the peers connected to it.
Invite by Link
A unique access link and shared credentials let each party in. They authenticate with a cryptographic keypair derived from their password — the same concept as a Web3 wallet, with no account created on any server. They see only their obligations.
Signed Contract Thread
All parties communicate through a live peer feed — confirmations, document uploads, and updates. Every message is cryptographically signed, timestamped, and replicated across the network.
